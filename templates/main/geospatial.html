GRRASP: Geospatial Risk and Resilience Assessment Platform for Pan European Critical Infrastructure Protection

The Geospatial Risk and Resilience Assessment Platform uses geo-spatial technologies for the analysis of the data and the visualization of the output obtained from spatial analysis. The system can be deployed in various servers in different EU Member States each one performing different tasks or accommodate different data.

SPARC: mixing historical data and spatial analysis to assess people at risk of flooding

The WFP Emergency Preparedness Branch (OSEP) developed a Spatial Risk Calendar (SPARC) to support emergency preparedness and programme planning. To support emergency preparedness and early warning SPARC combines data layers covering hazard exposure and population vulnerability to determine baseline probabilistic levels of humanitarian impact associated with particular hazard types, on a monthly basis, at a sub-national level, around the globe.
